The Traveller Map - Credits

For a more thorough listing of official and unofficial publications, see Traveller sector information by Don McKinney.

Explanation of acronyms and terminology: FFE = Far Future Enterprises; GDW = Game Designers' Workshop; DGP = Digest Group Publications. A "dot-map" is a high-level overview of one or more sectors that provides the location of star systems and allegiances, but no further details.

Official Traveller Universe

The Official Traveller Universe is comprised of materials published by licensees, although these may be decanonized or overwritten by later materials. The following section is presented in roughly chronological order to illustrate the evolution of the OTU data corpus over time.

Sectors of the Third Imperium

Old Expanses sector designed by Jim Cunningham and Gordon Sheridan. Portions of Old Expanses sector originally appeared in High Passage Magazine.

Reaver's Deep sector was designed by J. Andrew Keith. Portions of Reaver's Deep sector appeared in High Passage Magazine, and in Far Traveller Magazine. Additional data and adventures set in Reaver's Deep sector were published by Gamelords, Ltd.

Two subsectors of Reft Sector were designed by John Harshman, and appeared in Traveller Adventure 5: Trillion Credit Squadron, published by GDW.

Solomani Rim sector was designed by John Harshman and appears in Traveller Supplement 10, The Solomani Rim, published by GDW.

The Spinward Marches were designed by Marc W. Miller and appear in Traveller Supplement 3, The Spinward Marches, published by GDW.

Two subsectors of Trojan Reach sector were designed by Bob McWilliams and appear in Traveller Adventure 4, Leviathan, published by GDW.

The following 35 sectors were mapped in the Atlas of the Imperium: The Spinward Marches, Deneb, Corridor, Vland, Lishun, Antares, The Empty Quarter, Trojan Reach, Reft Sector, Gushemege, Dagudashag, Core, Fornast, Ley Sector, Riftspan Reaches*, Verge, Ilelish, Zarushagar, Massila, Delphi, Glimmerdrift Reaches, Hlakhoi*, Ealiyasiyw*, Reaver's Deep, Daibei, Diaspora, Old Expanses, Hinterworlds, Staihaia'yo*, Iwahfuah*, Dark Nebula*, Magyar, Solomani Rim, Alpha Crucis, Spica*. The Atlas of the Imperium was designed by Marc W. Miller, with assistance from Leroy W. L. Guatney and John Harshman for star system names, and was published by GDW. (Sectors marked with * do not contain worlds of the Third Imperium.)

Ten subsectors of Trojan Reach sector were further detailed by Mike Jackson and appeared in Third Imperium fanzine.

Corridor sector was further detailed by Marc W. Miller in The Short Adventures - Memory Alpha, published by FFE.

Several sectors were further detailed in issues of The Travellers' Digest published by DGP, by James Holden and other authors. These include: Deneb (James Holden, Mike Mikesh, and Nancy Parker, #1, #2, #19), Corridor (James Holden, Phillip Athans, Mike Mikesh, and David Riddell, #3, #18), Vland (Nancy Parker and J. Andrew Keith, #4, #5), Lishun (#6, #7), Core (#8, #9, #10), Massilia (Nancy Parker and Robert Parker, #11), Old Expanses (Nancy Parker and Robert Parker, #12), Magyar (#14), Daibei (#15), Reaver's Deep (#16), Dark Nebula (#17), Ealiyasiyw (James Holden, #18), Riftspan Reaches (James Holden, #19), Trojan Reach (James Holden, Mike Jackson, and Clay Bush, #20), Reft (James Holden, John Meyers, and Michael R. Mikesh, #20), Zarushagar (Greg Videll, #21).

The four sectors of the Domain of Deneb - Spinward Marches, Deneb, Trojan Reach, Reft were fully detailed (for the 1120 era) in the MegaTraveller Journal #1 (map) and #3 (full details), by Joe Fugate, published by DGP. Gateway was detailed in the MegaTraveller Journal #4, by William H. Keith, Jr., published by DGP.

Hinterworlds sector was further detailed by Rob Caswell and Karl Johnson and appeared in Challenge #39, published by GDW.

Subsector E of Antares, of Shurlarlem was detailed in Challenge #69.

Vland sector was further detailed by Joe Fugate and appeared in Vilani and Vargr, published by DGP.

Massilia sector was further detailed by Joe Fugate and appeared in Knightfall, published by GDW.

Six subsectors of Dagudashag sector were further detailed by Duncan Law-Green, Leighton Piper and Jae Campbell and appear in Signal GK fanzine.

The 35 sectors detailed in The Atlas of the Imperium had further data generated by Joe Fugate of DGP and this data set, incorporating details from the later publications listed above, was made available via the GEnie information service in 1989. The unofficial "History of the Imperium Working Group" (HIWG) comprised of Traveller fans including authors such as Michael R. Mikesh, David Burden and Leighton Piper, added additional data such as world and subsector names.

Diaspora sector was further detailed by Mark "Geo" Gelinas Sr. and appeared Astrogator's Guide to Diaspora Sector, published by GDW. Additional Diaspora sector information appeared in Geo's Terra Traveller TIMES fanzine.

Ley and Glimmerdrift Reaches sectors were further detailed by Martin J. Dougherty and Hunter Gordon and appear in Gateway to Destiny, published by QuickLink Interactive.

The Empty Quarter sector was further detailed by Jason "Flynn" Kemp and Alvin Plummer and appears in Stellar Reaches fanzine.

Gushemege, Ilelish, Dagudashaag and Zarushagar sectors were revised by Martin J. Dougherty and appeared in Bearers of the Flame, published by ComStar Games.

Based on initial work by Robert Eaglestone, Jim Fetters, and Don McKinney, the Traveller 5 Second Survey is an ongoing process to correct and revise the data for the sectors with Imperial worlds. This builds on but in many cases supersedes previous published data for these sectors: Alpha Crucis, Antares, Core, Corridor, Dagudashag, Daibei, Delphi, Deneb, Diaspora, The Empty Quarter, Fornast, Glimmerdrift Reaches, Gushemege, Hinterworlds, Ilelish, Ley Sector, Lishun, Magyar, Massilia, Old Expanses, Reaver's Deep, Reft Sector, Solomani Rim, The Spinward Marches, Trojan Reach, Verge, Vland, Zarushagar

Mongoose Publishing has released several sourcebooks based on this evolving data, including: The Spinward Marches by Martin J. Dougherty, Deneb Sector by Rob Eaglestone, Alien Module 1: Aslan by Gareth Hanrahan, Reft Sector by Martin Dougherty, Solomani Rim Sector by David Pulver, Alien Module 5: Solomani by David Pulver.

Several sectors beyond the Third Imperium were further detailed in issues of The Travellers' Digest published by DGP, by James Holden and other authors. These include: Dark Nebula (#17), Ealiyasiyw (#18), Riftspan Reaches (#19),

Mendan sector was designed by Michael R. Mikesh and appeared in Challege #49, published by GDW.

Gateway sector was designed by William H. Keith, Jr. and appeared in MegaTraveller Journal #4. It was revisited by Martin J. Dougherty and Hunter Gordon and appeared in Gateway to Destiny, published by QuickLink Interactive.

Crucis Margin sector was designed by Martin J. Dougherty and Hunter Gordon and appeared in Gateway to Destiny, published by QuickLink Interactive.

Ziafrplians sector was designed by Donald McKinney and appeared in Alien Module 4: Zhodani, published by Mongoose Publishing.

Referee's Preserve

Foreven sector has been set aside as a "referee's preserve," with no further official development to be done within the sector. Stellar positions were developed by Greg Videll and Mike Mikesh for the GDW newsletter Imperial Lines. See FFE Foreven Sector Reserve.

This site uses a version of Foreven sector designed by James "GypsyComet" Kundert, Dylan Lee and P-O "BeRKA" Bergstedt and appears at The Zhodani Base, but it should be emphasized that this data does not represent the Official Traveller Universe.

Unofficial Data

While the following sectors are named in the OTU, the sector data described below and used on this site has never been published by a Traveller licensee and is in no way official.

Spinward Sectors

The rimward eight subsectors of Far Frontiers/Afachtiabr sector were designed by Dale L. Kemper for FASA, and later featured in Ares magazine, Special Edition #2 published by TSR, and The Traveller Chronicle #2, #3, and #4 The coreward eight subsectors were designed by James "GypsyComet" Kundert, originally published in The Traveller Chronicle #5, #6 and #8.

The Beyond and Vanguard Reaches sectors were designed by Chuck Kallenbach II and appeared in publications of the same names by Paranoia Press. These licensed publications were "Approved For Use With Traveller" by GDW but are not considered part of the Official Traveller Universe.

Theta Borealis was designed by Bill Bledsaw, Pixie Bledsaw, Mark Holmer, Jeff Johnson, Debi Summerlott and Marc Summerlot, published as Theta Borealis Sector by Group One. This licensed publications was "Approved For Use With Traveller" by GDW but is not considered part of the Official Traveller Universe.

Waroatahe and Karleaya sectors designed by "jaymin" and Jeff Zeitlin, and published at The Zhodani Base.

Harea and Tlyasea sectors were designed and submitted by "77topaz", published at explorerbase.

Borders and Routes

Where possible, polity borders and communication/trade routes shown on maps in previous publications have been used. Unlike the raw system data, there is no standard data format for representing these. Where not previously available, borders have been automatically generated - see Improved Automatic Border Generation for TRAVELLER Sectors.

Antares and Fornast sector routes were designed by Jeff Zeitlin, from The Zhodani Base.

Delphi routes designed by Thomas Jones-Low, for the Journal of the Travellers' Aid Society.

Routes in the Julian Protectorate for the Amdukan, Mendan, Ingukrax and Star's End sectors were designed and submitted by Mike Kuehn.
